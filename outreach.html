Outreach & Science Comics

Visual storytelling, mentorship, and community education

I’ve always believed that science should be engaging, visual, and accessible — whether I’m mentoring students in the field or designing comics to explain microbiology to kids. Here are a few of my favorite outreach projects:

Comic Books & Visual Storytelling

  • Las Bacterias
    A comic explaining the role of bacteria in health and the environment, shared in Ecuadorian classrooms.
  • Sanitation & Poultry Comic
    Illustrated guide for zoonotic disease prevention, used in collaboration with local public health teams.
  • Lyme Disease Comic
    Created for Michigan public schools to teach kids about tick prevention and ecology.
  • Dinosaur Adventure!
    Activity sheet designed for the University of Michigan’s Natural History Museum to support informal learning.

Mentorship & STEM Education

  • Mentored 10+ undergraduate, graduate, and international students in R, spatial modeling, microbiology, and field design.
  • Supported multiple thesis projects and research fellowships, often working with first-gen and underrepresented students in science.

Talks, Events & Community Engagement

  • Guest lecturer at University of Michigan, Duke/OTS, and multiple public STEM programs (WEMOCO, Native youth STEM).
  • Organized and moderated panels on Indigenous science, climate, and public health education.
  • Volunteered as a Science Olympiad coach and judged local science fairs and outreach expos.

Note: If you'd like to view or adapt any of the comic books or activity sheets, feel free to reach out. I'm happy to share digital or print-ready versions.
